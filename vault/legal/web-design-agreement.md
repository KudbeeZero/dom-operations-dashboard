## 6. Who owns the finished website — **you do**
This is the whole point, so I'm spelling it out the way the industry does it (two overlapping
mechanisms, so ownership is airtight):

- **Work made for hire.** To the extent the law allows, the website and its deliverables are
  created as a "work made for hire" for you.
- **Assignment as backup.** Because "work made for hire" doesn't cleanly cover every kind of
  website work under U.S. copyright law, I **also assign to you, in advance, all of my right,
  title, and interest** in the finished website and its related materials. This assignment is
  the part that actually guarantees you own it.
- **Trigger: payment in full.** This ownership transfer takes effect **upon my receipt of
  payment in full.** Once you've paid in full, **you are the sole owner** of the finished
  website and its source files. You can use it, change it, move it, hand it to another
  developer, or not use it at all — it's yours. Until you've paid in full, the work remains
  mine (this is the only leverage I keep, and it's standard).
- **What I hand over on final payment:** all source files, images and assets I created for you,
  and every login needed to run and edit the site (see the handover checklist in the Domain &
  Hosting document).

### What I keep (small and optional)
- **Other people's tools.** Any third-party or open-source components (fonts, libraries,
  stock images, plugins) come with their own licenses. You get the right to use them as part
  of your site, but I can't "sell" you something I don't own — those stay under their own terms.
- **My reusable know-how.** General techniques, code snippets, and design patterns I reuse
  across projects stay mine to reuse. This never includes anything specific to your site,
  brand, or content.
- **Portfolio credit (optional).** I'd like to show your finished site as an example of my
  work and add a small "site by I Got A Dom" credit. **This is optional and only with your
  OK** — say no and it's gone, no hard feelings.

## 7. Your content and your responsibility
- You confirm you have the right to use the text, images, and logos you give me.
- You'll review the site and tell me if anything's wrong. Once you've approved it and paid in

## 8. No sensitive or regulated data
Don't put regulated or high-risk data into the project (patient/medical records, Social
Security numbers, full card or bank-account numbers, student records, privileged legal files).
See the **Acceptable Use & Sensitive Information** policy — it's part of this agreement.

## 9. Honest limits (no professional advice)
I build and clean up — I **don't** provide legal, tax, financial, or medical advice, and the
website I build for you isn't that kind of advice. If your site needs disclaimers or policies
of its own, get a professional to confirm them.

## 10. Fix-it promise
If something I delivered isn't what we agreed on, tell me and I'll fix it. I want you genuinely
happy with what you paid for.
