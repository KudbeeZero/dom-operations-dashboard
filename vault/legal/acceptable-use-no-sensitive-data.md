# Acceptable Use & Sensitive Information Policy

> ⚠️ **Plain-language template, not legal advice.** I Got A Dom is not a law firm and does
> not provide legal, tax, medical, or financial advice. Have a licensed Illinois attorney
> review and adapt this before you rely on it.

I clean up documents and build simple websites. To keep you safe and to keep me out of
regulated territory I have no business being in, there are a few kinds of information I
**can't** accept. This isn't me being difficult — it's me being honest about what a
one-person service should and shouldn't touch.

## The rule
**If your document contains any of the below, redact it (black it out / delete it) before
you send it, or don't send that document at all. I can't accept it.**

I follow a simple principle the FTC recommends for every business: don't collect or keep
sensitive personal information you don't have a real need for. The less of your sensitive
data exists in my hands, the safer you are. So I keep it to a minimum on purpose.

## What I can't take in
- **Medical or health records — anything that identifies a patient.** (This is "protected
  health information" under HIPAA. The moment a service handles it for a healthcare provider,
  it can get pulled into HIPAA as a "business associate." I stay out of that entirely by not
  accepting PHI — ever.)
- **Social Security numbers and government ID numbers** (driver's license, passport, etc.).
  The FTC names SSNs as one of the things identity thieves use most. I don't want them on my
  screen.
- **Full payment-card numbers or bank-account / routing numbers.** Card and financial-account
  data carries its own heavy rules (PCI, GLBA). If you're paying me, you do it through a
  normal payment method — you never send me the raw numbers.
- **Student education records** protected under FERPA.
- **Attorney-client or privileged legal documents.** Sending these to a third party can blow
  the privilege that protects you. Don't risk it.

## What I happily DO work with
Resumes, bios, cover letters, meeting notes, screenshots and photos with text in them, menus,
flyers, rough email drafts, voice-note transcriptions, scattered bullet points — the normal,
everyday "this is a mess and I need it clean" stuff. **If you're not sure whether something is
fine to send, ask me first. I'll tell you straight.**

## If sensitive info slips through
If you accidentally send me something on the refuse list, I'll tell you, I won't use it, and
I'll delete it. If a document is *mostly* fine but has one sensitive line, the cleanest fix is
to black that line out before sending.

## Honest scope
I organize facts, clean up writing, and create clear drafts. I **don't** give legal, tax,
financial, or medical advice, and nothing I produce should be treated as that kind of advice.
